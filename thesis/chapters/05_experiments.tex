\subsection{Building an estimator}

The most simple approach is the harmonic estimator, since

\begin{equation*}
    c_0(\eta) = \bE_{\theta\sim\pi_0}\qty[L_0(\theta\mid D_0)^\eta].
\end{equation*}

Then, we define $\delta_a$ as

\begin{equation}
    \delta_a(\eta, \theta_1, \dots, \theta_K) = \dfrac{1}{K} \sum_{i=1}^K L_0(\theta_i\mid D_0)^\eta
\end{equation}

And with this estimator we can then employ a Portkey Barker's~\cite{vats2021} to sample from the power posterior. 

This $\delta_a$ has a major two-sided feature, while it is easy to sample from the initial prior, its efficiency is low due to its large non-informativeness.

In order to improve the efficiency, we can produce an importance sampling estimator by concentrating the probability density around the $\hat{\theta}_{MLE}$, which gives us

\begin{equation*}
c_0(\eta) = \int_{\theta\in\Theta} L_0(\theta\mid D_0)^\eta \pi_0(\theta)\dd{\theta} = \bE_{\theta\sim\pi_{MLE}}\qty[L_0(\theta\mid D_0)^\eta\dfrac{\pi_0(\theta)}{\pi_{MLE}(\theta)}].    
\end{equation*}


We can then define $\delta_b$ as:

\begin{equation}
    \delta_b(\eta, \theta_1, \dots, \theta_K) = \dfrac{1}{K} \sum_{i=1}^K L_0(\theta_i\mid D_0)^\eta\dfrac{\pi_0(\theta)}{\pi_{MLE}(\theta)}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{The choice of $\lambda$ and $c$ for the Gaussian likelihood with known $\Sigma$.}

Considering the functional form of our estimator $\widehat{\Delta}(\eta, \eta') = (\eta' - \eta) \log L_0\qty(D_0\mid\widetilde{\theta})$ and that $L_0$ is the likelihood of $D_0 = \{d_{01}, \dots, d_{0N}\}$ where each $d_{0j} \sim \cN_d(\theta, \Sigma)$ for $j = 1, \dots, N$ with known $\Sigma$, we can find that 

$$\argmax_{\theta \in \Theta} \log L_0\qty(D_0\mid\theta) = \hat{\theta}_{\text{MLE}}= \dfrac{1}{N}\sum_{j=1}^N d_{0j},$$

and, therefore, we have that

$$\max_{\theta \in \Theta} \log L_0\qty(D_0\mid\theta) = -\frac{N d}{2}\log(2\pi) - \frac{N}{2}\log\det\Sigma - \frac{N}{2}\operatorname{tr}(\Sigma^{-1}S),$$

 where $S= \frac{1}{N}\sum_{j=1}^N(x_j - \hat{\theta}_{\text{MLE}})(x_j - \hat{\theta}_{\text{MLE}})^\top$

\begin{itemize}
  \item When data agree:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $\eta$ has well-distributed posterior mass.
    \item Importance and power estimators perform reasonably well.
    \item The prior estimator is highly inefficient and deviates substantially from the baseline. 
    \end{itemize}
    \item When data partially agree:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Posterior mass shifts toward smaller $\eta$.
    \item Power and importance estimators recover the baseline distribution well.
    \item The prior estimator exhibits slow mixing.
  \end{itemize}
  \item When data conflict:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Posterior concentrates near $\eta \approx 0$ (as expected theoretically).
    \item All estimators capture the shrinkage effect correctly.
    \item Differences across methods become smaller as the posterior sharpens.
  \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
